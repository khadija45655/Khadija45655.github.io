Project Overview
Apart of the For Inspiration and Recognition Of Science and Technology (FIRST) 2019-2020 season. In FIRST teams design, program, and build a robot starting with common set of rules to play in a themed head-to-head challenge. Teams also build a brand, develop community partnerships for support, and work to promote STEM in their local community.
My Contributions
In my final year on the team I was lead programmer and captain. I designed and implemented a new software structure, managed the programming team's deadlines, and updated clean documentation of our code. As the Captain, I scheduled and documented meetings, scheduled events in the local community, and led my team to the Ohio State Championship.
Awards:
  • Collins Aerospace Innovate
  • Think
  • Motivate
For the 2019-2020 season the challenge was to make a tower of 8-by-8 inch Lego cubes, and drag them across the field. During the autonomous period, we had to recognize a certain block that had a picture on it, and stack it on the tower. In order to accomplish this our team implemented computer vision in order to recognize the block to pick it up. We created a bitmap of the field in black and white in order for the robot to be able to distinguish between the blocks.

Our robot went through multiple iterations for each mechanism before arriving at the design seen above. From shaky slides, to a weak intake, we developed an amazing final product.
